Parrots , also known as psittacines, are birds with a strong curved beak, upright stance, and clawed feet. They areconformed by four families that contain roughly 410 species in 101 genera, found mostly intropical and subtropical regions. The four families are the Psittaculidae,Psittacidae, Cacatuoidea, and Strigopidae. One-third of all parrot species are threatened by extinction,with a higher aggregate extinction risk than any other comparable bird group. Parrots have a generally pantropical distribution with several speciesinhabiting temperate regions as well. The greatest diversity of parrots is in South America and Australasia.
